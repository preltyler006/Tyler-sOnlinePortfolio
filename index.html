About Me - Tyler Prellwitz

We all have goals, but how do you reach them? You have to work for them because they won’t just be willed into existence. My goal is to become a software engineer in the future. I am currently working toward that goal by learning some languages in my computer science class, including Python, HTML, CSS, and some JavaScript (outside of class). I have chosen this career option because I have always found that coding allows me to express my creativity in a way that I am unable to do with other art forms. My plans to achieve this goal are to dedicate my time to not just learning about other languages in the computer science world, but rather how to learn them faster and more efficiently. Coding languages will not stay the same, and in this industry, you have to be able to adapt quickly.

To help me along this path, I have acquired certain skills that will help me greatly. I have been figuring out the best way for me to learn efficiently, which is through extra and in-depth note-taking, as well as lots and lots of practice with the languages that I am learning. For example, in my math class, instead of just mindlessly writing down the notes and tossing them once class is over, once I get home I go back over the notes in order to make sure I completely understand them and use them to my advantage when doing homework. I have also learned to type relatively quickly, which is very useful when there is a lot to write out in code.

Not everything in the industry is about coding or note-taking or learning, however. You need to be able to collaborate well with others and know how and when to be a leader when the time comes. I have been captain of my hockey team and been able to take command and tell everyone what they need to do and when they need to do it. Being a good collaborator is also very important because you have to realize that you don’t know everything, and other people will have good and useful ideas. Having two minds is better than one, especially when you can communicate with them well. I have demonstrated this throughout the numerous projects in my computer science class, as well as now in my engineering class. I am very capable of leading, learning, and listening in order to get work done.

App Development: Creative Expression

Course: Computer Science Essentials
Duration: 2 Months (Mid September 2020 - Mid November 2020)
Objective: Create an android app that highlights a hot topic that more people should be aware of.

The hot topic that my partner (Samith Ganesh) and I decided to create an app about was the California wildfires, which was a big topic at the time of making the app. We came up with an idea of having a few tress on fire on the screen, and the user would have to click them to put out the fires. The trees would move slowly on level 1, and slowly would move faster and faster until the final level, where they would move extremely fast. My partner and I worked very well together, communicating effectively inside and outside of class hours. We got our work done on time, and if one of us had a question, we wouldn’t hesitate to ask for help. My role in the project was to add the smaller but important features such as the score counter and the timer at the bottom, as well as styling the app to make it user friendly. We got feedback from our peers multiple times throughout the project, such as adding a border to see where the boundaries for the trees were, and how they would overlap sometimes. We fixed these bugs by adding more “if then” statements to our code, however my emulator was unable to handle all ofthe code because it was an older model. If we had more time to work on the project, we would have made the app more friendly for all devices so that everyone could run it. Our documentation for this project is here, as well as the link to the AIA file for the code here.

Project 1.6 Compound Machine Design "XTRA"

Course: Honors Principles Of Engineering
Duration: 2 Weeks (September 14 2021 - September 28 2021)
Objective: Design, build, and test a prototype to pour water for members of the compound. Our machine will transfer liquid from one cup to another

My partners for this project were Ayush Guha and Sundeep Kalinidi. My role in the project was designing our final prototype as well as building the pulley and cradle system, as well as testing the water spillage and limiting that factor. Entrepreneurship Project 2021-2022

Course: Honors Principles Of Engineering
Duration: 2 Months (March 17 2022 - May 18 2022)
Objective: Design and/or build & test a product to be evaluated by classmates and judges for potential payout in engineering-wide competition. Needs to utilize a topic/skill learned in current year of engineering class, and the product needs to address/solve a current issue/problem in the world.

My partners for this project were Rishit Balaji and Maxwell Vu, both in POE 3rd period with McGarrah. Our problem we attempted to solve was a lack of sanitation in gyms in current times, still in the pandemic from early 2020. Proper cleaning of equipment inbetween uses was near nonexistent, only being cleaned after many gyms have been closed. Our solution was a Dumbbell-Cleaning UV Machine, which would utilize UV-C light from wavelengths 200nm-239nm. At this wavelength, the UV light would disinfect dumbbell grips by eliminating bacteria & viruses (including COVID-19), but still be safe for humans as it would not penetrate human skin. I modeled a CAD prototype for our prodcut, as well as a circuit demonstrating how our product would be wired, both in Tinkercad. I utilized my knowledge from our circuits unit in order to understand the schematic used to create the circuit. You can find our in depth documentation for the project here.

Influential Speakers

On December 8th 2021, I attended the speaker event with Donald James as he talked about his career in NASA. He was employed in 1982 and retired in 2017, meaning he worked at NASA for 35 years!. His job was to inform kids and teens about things concerning space travel through NASA. In 1986 however, after the Challenger II accident, he went around to schools to talk about what went so wrong and help preserve their image. Throughout his speech, the topic of perserverence and manners came up many time, as he had written a book called "Manners Will Take You Where Brains and Money Won't: Wisdom from Momma and 35 Years at NASA". His presentation had a very good outlook on the engineering career industry, and how you always should be focused on being good at your job. However, being respectful and having good manners will get you farther in life than just your brain will.

On February 16, 2022, I attended a speaker presentation by Kimberli Zhong and she talked about her career through computer science. She was the first graduating class of DHS to be in the engineering academy in 2014. She studied computer science at MIT and moved back to the bay area to join a few internships starting with Google, then to Airbnb, and now at a startup named "Applied Intuition". At her job now, she designs simulations for testing vehicles before uploading the software to self driving cars. Currently, she leads an engineering team in her company of about 7 people, making her way up the ranks after starting with the company from their early days. This speaker was very inspirational to me because she was an Asian American woman, being able to thrive in this career while facing hints of sexism and racism.

On April 13, 2022, I went to a speaker presentation by Kathleen Morse. She is a materials science expert, and is the founder & major owner of Yosemite Space. She got her bachelor from MIT and finished her Masters and PHD at Stanford. Her company designs small computers to be put into space on rockets that are able to withstand and prevent single event effects. These effects are tiny radiation particles messing with electrons, which can be catastrophic if hit in the wrong place at the wrong time. These small computers have "gumstix" cpus in them, and as the title suggests, they are the size of a gumstick. These cpus when put in space experienced no single event effects, proving their usefulness and potency.
